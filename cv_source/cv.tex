\section{Summary}
I am a PhD researcher at University College Cork working on language modelling and visualisation for parallel text analysis, with a focus on how texts change across translation, paraphrase, adaptation, simplification, and machine generation. My research combines NLP, large language models, similarity metrics, and interactive visual analytics to study relationships between related texts in ways that are measurable and interpretable.

I am developing enrichment pipelines, scoring methods, and visual interfaces for difficult text-pair analysis, especially cases where surface similarity is low but meaningful textual relationships remain. The goal is not only to detect text reuse, but to help researchers explain how and how much two texts diverge.

Alongside the PhD I bring four years of industry experience in data analytics and BI engineering (Azure Databricks, Power BI, Python), academic teaching at UCC and in Sri Lanka, and a current secondment at Nimbus Research Centre on the inTrusted project, where I am examining trust and intentionality in agentic AI systems.
